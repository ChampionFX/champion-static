1. Account currency

Is the currency of the funds accepted in your trading account.

2. Leverage

Is the ability to control a large amount of money using a minimal capital from yourself. Where does the rest of the money come from? Your trusted broker provides the rest.

Let’s say you fork out a capital of USD 50 and we’re offering a leverage of 1:1,000. How much money, or margin, is available to you when you start trading?

USD 50 x 1,000 = USD 50,000 (is the margin available)

3. Order execution

Typically comes in two varieties: market execution and instant execution. With market execution, you place an order at the broker’s price. You agree on the price in advance. There are no requotes with market execution.

What about instant execution? In this case, your order is place at the price that’s available at that time. Requotes are possible if the price fluctuates a great deal before the order execution is complete.

4. Spread

Is the difference between the buy price and sell price. When you enter into a trade, you are buying an asset that you will be selling at a later time or date for a profit or loss. The difference in price is called a spread. A variable spread means that the spread will remain as it is no matter the market condition. A tight spread and our tightest spread means that the spread may increase depending on market conditions, as it is subject to market volatility caused by news events.

5. Commission

Refers to the commission fees that we charge for each trade you place with us. ChampionFX currently charges no commission for all accounts.

6. Margin call

Refers to the state where the remaining funds in your account is unable to cover the leverage or margin requirement. You can deposit additional funds to prevent a margin call escalating to a stop out level.

7. Stop out level

Is the level where your margin ratio has become unsustainable, and we issue a forced closure of pending orders, and any open positions. A stop out level usually results from a high negative balance in your trading account.

8. Hedged margin

Refers to the funds required to open and maintain a position on the same asset, but in opposite directions.

9. Maximum ticket size

Refers to the maximum volume of lots allowed per trade.
